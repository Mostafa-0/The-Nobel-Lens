Alfred Nobel: The Visionary Founder

The Nobel Prizes are widely regarded as the most prestigious awards given in various fields. They were established by the will of Swedish inventor Alfred Nobel and first awarded in 1901.

  • Alfred Nobel (1833-1896), inventor of dynamite, transformed industries. A twist of fate (and a misread obituary) spurred him to use his wealth to fund the prestigious Nobel Prizes, a lasting testament to peace and progress.

  • Since 1895, the Nobel Prize honors groundbreaking minds in science, literature, chemistry, physics, medicine and peace. These visionary pioneers, the embodiment of "Nobel character," propel humanity forward, just as Nobel himself envisioned.

  • More than laureates, the "Nobel character" embodies a legacy. It's the relentless pursuit of progress, excellence, and positive impact a vision ignited by Alfred Nobel and reflected in every Prize winner.

Pharaohs of Knowledge: Meet Egypt's Nobel Laureates

Anwar Sadat

Anwar Sadat

Nobel Prize: Peace, 1978

Achievement: Anwar El-Sadat was awarded the Nobel Prize For their efforts to achieve peace in the Middle East, particularly for their decisive contribution to the conclusion of a peace treaty between Israel and Egypt . He regained Sinai after it was occupied by Israel during the Six-Day War in 1967.

Naguib Mahfouz

Naguib Mahfouz

Nobel Prize: Literature, 1988

Achievement: Naguib Mahfouz was awarded Nobel prize for his enriched Arabic writing for fifty years without interruption with his works of fiction and short stories, which represent the human condition with realism and depth.He remains the only Arab writer to have received the Nobel Prize in Literature

Ahmed Zewail

Ahmed Zewail

Nobel Prize: Chemistry, 1999

Achievement: Ahmed Zewail was awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his pioneering work on femtochemistry, which enabled the observation of chemical reactions on extremely short timescales.He is known as the “father of Femtochemistry.”

Mohamed El Baradei

Mohamed El Baradei

Nobel Prize: Peace, 2005

Achievement: Mohamed ElBaradei, served as the Director General of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA). He and the IAEA were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to prevent nuclear energy from being used for military purposes and to ensure its peaceful use.
